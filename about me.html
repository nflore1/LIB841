About Me

Who Am I?

I am from the island of Guam, located in the western Pacific. However, I was mostly raised in Kentucky in communities comprised of people from around the world. These experiences ignited my passion for languages and cultures and led me to a career in teaching English language learners of all ages. As I continue to advance my education and career path, I aspire to be a school librarian who is a resource for adolescents, especially multilingual learners and those from marginalized communities. I desire to provide them with access to the information they need to be successful and ensure they see themselves represented in the literature they choose to read. I am motivated in my goals so that my three children, who are indigenous K'iche' Maya from Guatemala in addition to their Pacific Islander heritage, see libraries and the treasured books they contain as welcoming, inclusive spaces.
